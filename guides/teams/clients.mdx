share the same conversations, docs, and agents.

## Using the client

Everything a team does day to day — chat and collaborative docs — happens in the client. See
[Use chat and docs](/guides/teams/use-chat-and-docs). @-mentioning a bot is how you bring a
an [agent](/concepts/the-agent-model) into a conversation.

## Building the mobile apps from source

<Warning>
  The Android and iOS repos ship with a **placeholder identifier** (`com.example.octo`) and are
  **not** provisioned for any store listing — you cannot build a signed, distributable app as-is.
  Before building your fork, replace the placeholder with your own reverse-DNS identifier
  everywhere (build config, manifests/entitlements, `google-services.json` /
  `GoogleService-Info.plist`, package declarations). Each repo's README lists every file to change.
</Warning>

## Admins get a separate console

Operators manage tenants, orgs, users, and channels in the
[admin console](/guides/teams/admin-console) (`octo-admin`), a separate internal/VPN-only bundle —
not the end-user client.
