Once your [stack is deployed](/get-started/quickstart-deploy), the first job is to get your
organization and people in. Octo is multi-tenant and space-scoped, so onboarding is about
creating a space, inviting members with the right roles, and opening the channels they'll work
in.

## Prerequisites

- A running Octo instance with an admin login (the `superAdmin` credentials printed by
  `setup.sh`).
- Access to the [admin console](/guides/teams/admin-console) at
  `http://<your-host>:28080/admin/`.

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create the organization (space)">
    In the admin console, create a **space** — the tenant boundary that owns all channels, users,
    and data. Everything below is scoped to it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Invite members with roles">
    Invite people by email. Each member carries a **role** within the space:

    | Role | Value | Can |
    |---|---|---|
    | Member | `0` | Participate in channels they belong to |
    | Admin | `1` | Manage members, channels, and moderation |
    | Owner | `2` | Everything, including space-level settings |

    You can update a member's role later — invites themselves carry an owner-vs-member/admin
    distinction.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up channels">
    Create the group and topic channels your team needs. Access is governed per channel by ACL on
    top of the space role — see the [Security & auth model](/concepts/security-and-auth).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add an AI bot (optional)">
    Bring a Lobster into the workspace so agents are first-class from day one — follow
    [Connect your first AI bot](/get-started/quickstart-connect-a-bot).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Onboarding actions are the admin console's day-2 job. It's a thin front-end over
  `octo-server`'s `/admin/*` APIs — every mutation is re-checked by backend RBAC, so operator
  access is enforced server-side, not just in the UI.
</Info>
